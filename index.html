Research Overview

We conducted interviews with six university students to understand their perspectives on "Green Life" and sustainable living practices. This research aimed to explore the importance of environmental consciousness, individual contributions, challenges faced, and advice for promoting a greener lifestyle.

Key Findings

1. What is green life in your country?

Most interviewees define "Green Life" as an eco-friendly lifestyle that minimizes negative impacts on nature by using resources in a sustainable way.

2. What is the importance of green life?

Respondents emphasize that "Green Life" helps protect the environment, reduce pollution, improve air and water quality, and increase community awareness of sustainability. Some also mention its long-term health benefits.

3. What did you do to contribute to the green life?

Common actions include recycling waste, using reusable products, conserving energy, planting trees, and joining environmental campaigns. Some people also opt for eco-friendly transportation, such as bicycles or public transit.

4. What is the most difficult when doing a green life?

Frequently mentioned challenges include difficulty changing consumption habits, the higher cost of eco-friendly products, lack of government or corporate support, and general apathy from certain segments of the public.

5. Do you have any advice for everyone to contribute to making a green life?

Suggestions include starting with small changes, like bringing reusable bags when shopping, using personal water bottles, and saving electricity and water. Interviewees also encourage early environmental education and participation in community activities to raise awareness.

Who were the interviewees?

The interviewees included six university students. Their backgrounds influenced their perspectives; for instance, the students focused on cost-effective and practical ways to live sustainably.

Analysis of Results

Differences Between Answers

Some interviewees focus on personal actions—such as changing daily habits. These differences may arise from their distinct life experiences and roles: for example, whereas students often concentrate on more immediate, budget-friendly approaches.

Similarities in Answers

Most interviewees agree on the importance of reducing single-use plastics, saving energy, and raising public awareness. They also share the belief that individual efforts can significantly impact the environment. This consensus likely stems from common exposure to environmental campaigns, social media discussions, and educational programs that stress the urgency of sustainable living.

Our Opinion

What we found interesting

Our group finds the idea of combining individual efforts particularly inspiring—where everyone makes small changes in their daily routines while also advocating for broader policy reforms. We also appreciate the interviewees' emphasis on community engagement, as it encourages more people to adopt a sustainable lifestyle together.
